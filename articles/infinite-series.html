The Mysteries of the Infinite

A sample page showing prose and mathematics

This is an example article page. Write ordinary prose in HTML and place LaTeX-style mathematics between MathJax delimiters.

Inline mathematics sits inside a sentence like this: \(a_n = 1/n\). A displayed equation gets its own centered line:

\[ 1 + \frac12 + \frac13 + \frac14 + \cdots \]

Compare that series with a geometric series:

\[ 1 + \frac12 + \frac14 + \frac18 + \cdots = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\left(\frac12\right)^k = 2. \]

A small argument

The partial sum of the first \(n+1\) terms is

\[ S_n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} r^k = \frac{1-r^{n+1}}{1-r}, \qquad r \ne 1. \]

When \(|r|<1\), the power \(r^{n+1}\) tends to zero, leaving \(S_n \to 1/(1-r)\).

Keep the HTML plain. Let typography, spacing, and mathematics do most of the visual work.
